atBash Cipher


Usage

The atBash cipher is used by switching characters for the reverse in the alphabet

The atbash cipher is another substitution Cipher

The cipher was origionally used to encode the hebrew alphabet
however it can be modified to be used with any simple language.

The cipher is known as a monoalphabetic cipher, to encode you simple take the alphabet
and swich each character with the reverse in the alphabet. So "a" would become "z"
and "z" would become "a".

Due to it's simplicity the cipher has 0 communications security.

The History

The name comes from the hebrew language, and follows the encoding style.
It takes the first, last, second then second last Hebrew letters,
(Aleph-Tav-Beth-Shin) which loosely managed to come out at atBash.

There is many refernces to atBash in the bible, multiple times people have
used atbash to cover up city names.
This is done in "Jeremiah 51:1 - 'Behold, I will raise up against babylon, and against the inhabitants of Lev-kamai'"
- Lev-kamai meaning Chaldeans after atbash is applied
